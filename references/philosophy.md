# Retardmaxxing — the philosophy behind the skill

Load this only when the user asks about the philosophy itself. It is background, not instructions.

## Definition

Retardmaxxing is an internet philosophy of taking action without overthinking. Core claim: the primary obstacle to output is not lack of intelligence or planning but analysis paralysis — recursive pre-action deliberation, perfectionism, and fear of judgment. Practitioners deliberately suppress that loop to maximize output velocity and instinct-driven decisions.

Proponents frame it explicitly as *not* recklessness: the target is pre-action overthinking, and the philosophy presupposes baseline competence and judgment. "Not overthinking" ≠ "not thinking."

It belongs to the "-maxxing" family (looksmaxxing, gymmaxxing) but is a meta-turn: it maximizes the *absence* of a cognitive behavior — optimization by subtraction.

## Origin

Earliest documented use: September 2018 on Looksmax.org forums. Popularized as a philosophy by YouTuber Elisha Long (~251k subscribers, "High Thumos Men's Group") starting September 2025. Went viral late 2025 on TikTok and X, hit mainstream discourse early 2026. Marc Andreessen endorsed it on an a16z podcast in March 2026 ("he's like my new life coach"), drawing criticism from Paul Graham and Seth Bannon and agreement from Elon Musk.

Signature phrases:

- "Throw shit at the wall and see what sticks" — start now, even messy
- "Do a 360 and moonwalk away" — cut what drains you; abandon sunk costs
- "Your gut's smarter than your brain's endless debates" — honor the first impulse

## Core principles

1. Action over optimization — action breeds inspiration and clarity, not the reverse
2. Honor the first impulse — the first read is the honest one; deliberation is the social brain editing it into something safer (caveat: recurring impulses, basic judgment retained)
3. Decouple from judgment — most pre-action anxiety is about how the action will look
4. Imperfection is the baseline — every first attempt is supposed to be rough
5. Focus on the task, detach from the outcome
6. Kill rehearsal behavior — pre-writing, pre-simulating reactions, replaying scenarios
7. Iterate against reality — test judgment against the world, not internal simulation

## Psychological grounding

- Action bias (behavioral economics): actors outperform deliberators in many domains
- Analysis paralysis / paradox of choice (Schwartz): more evaluated options → lower action rates
- Self-efficacy (Bandura): belief in ability is built by doing, not by thinking about doing
- Framed partly as a counter-movement to AI-era cognitive outsourcing (MIT Media Lab "Your Brain on ChatGPT" study)

## Critiques this skill designs around

1. **The name.** "Retard" is a slur with real history of harm; ironic reclamation does not neutralize it. → The skill keeps the meme name internally but bans the slur from all generated artifacts and user-facing text.
2. **Stakes-blindness.** Fine for breaking inertia; dangerous for consequential, irreversible decisions. → The judgment gate: full-send on reversible actions, mandatory confirmation on irreversible or high-blast-radius ones.
3. **Cover for carelessness.** The language sometimes collapses "not overthinking" into "not thinking." → Bias-to-action applies to starting and iterating, never to skipping verification.
4. **Tokenmaxxing counter-evidence.** Headline 80–90% AI code acceptance collapses to 10–30% once churn and rework are counted; the "80% problem" — agents ship the happy path and omit error handling, retries, security. → Ship standard: velocity on drafts, discipline on merges, tests run before "done."
